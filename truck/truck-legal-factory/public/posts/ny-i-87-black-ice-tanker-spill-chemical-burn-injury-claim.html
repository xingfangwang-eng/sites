Evidence Collection Checklist

Here is a checklist of evidence to collect in the event of a hazardous materials accident:

1. Medical Records

* Consult with a doctor as soon as possible to document injuries and treatment. * Keep a record of all medical treatment, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and surgeries. * Obtain copies of medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.

2. Police Reports

* Obtain a copy of the police report, including witness statements and vehicle damage assessments. * Take photos of the accident scene, including damage to vehicles and property.

3. Witness Statements

* Obtain witness statements, including contact information and descriptions of the accident. * Take photos of witnesses, including their vehicles and any relevant documentation.

4. Video Footage

* Obtain video footage of the accident, including surveillance footage and dashcam footage. * Take photos of the accident scene, including damage to vehicles and property.

5. Property Damage

* Obtain photos of property damage, including vehicle damage and property damage. * Keep a record of any repairs or replacements made to property.

6. Financial Records

* Keep a record of all financial losses, including medical expenses, lost wages, and property damage. * Obtain copies of financial records, including bank statements, pay stubs, and tax returns.

7. Insurance Policy

* Obtain a copy of the insurance policy, including coverage amounts and policy terms. * Review the policy to ensure that it covers the accident and any resulting injuries or property damage.

Legal Statutes

In the United States, several federal and state laws govern truck accidents, including:

* The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSA) at 49 CFR 392.7, which requires truck drivers to maintain a safe following distance and avoid cutting off other vehicles. * The Motor Carrier Act of 1935 (49 U.S.C. § 13102), which governs the operation of commercial motor vehicles. * State traffic laws, such as the Uniform Vehicle Code (UVC), which requires trucks to be equipped with underride guards to prevent accidents like Mary's.

These laws provide a foundation for victims like Mary to seek compensation for their injuries and damages.

5 Professional FAQs

Q: What are the most common causes of truck underride accidents?

A: The most common causes of truck underride accidents include:

* Driver error, such as speeding or failure to maintain a safe following distance * Vehicle malfunction, such as brake failure or tire blowout * Poor maintenance or inspection of the truck or trailer * Failure to install or maintain underride guards

Q: What is the role of underride guards in preventing truck accidents?

A: Underride guards are designed to prevent cars from sliding under the rear of a truck in the event of a collision. They are typically installed on the rear of the truck and consist of a metal bar or plate that extends down from the truck's frame. When a car collides with the truck, the underride guard is designed to absorb the impact and prevent the car from slipping underneath.

Q: How do I determine the value of my truck underride accident claim?

A: The value of a truck underride accident claim is typically determined by the severity of the injuries, the cost of medical treatment, lost wages, and property damage. An experienced attorney can help you navigate the complex process of determining the value of your claim.

Q: Can I still seek compensation if I am partially at fault for the accident?

A: Yes, even if you are partially at fault for the accident, you may still be able to seek compensation. However, your compensation may be reduced proportionally to your degree of fault.

Q: How long do I have to file a truck underride accident claim?

A: The statute of limitations for filing a truck underride accident claim varies by state, but is typically between 1-3 years from the date of the accident. It is essential to seek professional representation as soon as possible to ensure that your claim is filed within the applicable time limit.

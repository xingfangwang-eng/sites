Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. Here are five common tactics and countermeasures:

1. Denial of Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability for accidents, claiming that the victim was partially or fully responsible. Countermeasure: Gather evidence, including witness statements, police reports, and expert testimony, to establish liability. 2. Undervaluation of Damages: Insurance companies may undervalue damages, claiming that the victim's injuries are minor or that the costs of treatment are excessive. Countermeasure: Hire experts to assess the victim's injuries and estimate the costs of treatment. 3. Delaying Payments: Insurance companies may delay payments, citing the need for further investigation or the absence of necessary documentation. Countermeasure: Send a demand letter to the insurance company, outlining the required documentation and the expected timeline for payment. 4. Offering Low Settlements: Insurance companies may offer low settlements, hoping that the victim will accept the offer without further negotiation. Countermeasure: Negotiate with the insurance company, using expert testimony and evidence to support the victim's claims. 5. Using High-Pressure Sales Tactics: Insurance companies may use high-pressure sales tactics, such as threatening to cancel the policy or implying that the victim is at fault. Countermeasure: Remain calm and assertive, and do not engage with the insurance company's tactics. Instead, seek the advice of an attorney.

Module 7: Evidence Collection Checklist

Victims of truck accidents should collect the following evidence to support their claims:

* Photographs: Take photographs of the accident scene, including the damaged vehicles, skid marks, and any other relevant features. * Witness Statements: Collect witness statements, including their contact information and a detailed account of what they saw. * Police Reports: Obtain a copy of the police report, which will include information about the accident, including the time, location, and parties involved. * Medical Records: Collect medical records, including doctor's notes, hospital bills, and any other relevant documentation. * Expert Testimony: Hire experts to provide testimony about the accident, including the cause of the accident and the extent of the victim's injuries. * Log of Lost Wages: Keep a log of lost wages, including the date, time, and amount of lost income. * Property Damage Records: Keep records of property damage, including the date, time, and amount of damage. * Insurance Policies: Collect insurance policies, including the policy holder's name, policy number, and coverage amounts.

Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. In this module, we will expose five common insurance company tactics and provide countermeasures.

1. Delaying Payment: Insurance companies may delay payment to wear down the victim and increase the likelihood of settlement. Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of all correspondence and payment requests.

2. Denying Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability to avoid paying claims. Countermeasure: Gather evidence, including witness statements and physical evidence, to prove liability.

3. Undervaluing Claims: Insurance companies may undervalue claims to reduce the settlement amount. Countermeasure: Hire an appraiser to determine the fair market value of the claim.

4. Using Pre-Existing Condition Exclusions: Insurance companies may use pre-existing condition exclusions to deny claims. Countermeasure: Obtain a copy of the policy and review it carefully to determine if the exclusion applies.

5. Offering Low Settlements: Insurance companies may offer low settlements to save money. Countermeasure: Don't accept the first offer, and negotiate for a higher settlement.

Evidence Collection Checklist

In this module, we will provide a comprehensive checklist for evidence collection in truck accident cases.

1. Photograph the Scene: Take photographs of the accident scene, including any damage to the vehicles and surrounding property.

2. Gather Witness Statements: Collect witness statements, including names, contact information, and a detailed description of what they saw.

3. Obtain Police Reports: Obtain a copy of the police report, which may include information about the accident, including the parties involved and any injuries or damages.

4. Document Injuries: Document any injuries sustained during the accident, including medical records and witness statements.

5. Gather Physical Evidence: Collect any physical evidence related to the accident, including damaged property and vehicle parts.

6. Keep a Log: Keep a log of all correspondence and communication with the insurance company and any other parties involved.

7. Review Policy Documents: Review policy documents, including the insurance policy and any relevant riders or endorsements.

8. Consult with an Expert: Consult with an expert, such as a mechanic or an accident reconstructionist, to determine the cause of the accident and the extent of damages.

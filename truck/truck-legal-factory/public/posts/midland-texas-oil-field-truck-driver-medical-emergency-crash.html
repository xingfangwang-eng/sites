Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims for oil field truck driver medical emergency crashes. Some common tactics include:

1. Denial of Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability for the accident, even if there is clear evidence of their insured's fault. 2. Undervaluation of Damages: Insurance companies may undervalue the extent of the damages, including medical expenses, pain and suffering, lost wages, and property damage. 3. Delaying Payment: Insurance companies may delay payment of claims, hoping that the victim will settle for a lower amount. 4. Misconstruing Regulatory Requirements: Insurance companies may misinterpret or misconstrue regulatory requirements, such as those related to commercial driver's licenses or vehicle inspections. 5. Misrepresenting Medical Evidence: Insurance companies may misrepresent or manipulate medical evidence to support their denial of liability or reduction of damages.

Countermeasures

Seek Independent Medical Evaluation: Obtain an independent medical evaluation to determine the extent of the damages. 3.

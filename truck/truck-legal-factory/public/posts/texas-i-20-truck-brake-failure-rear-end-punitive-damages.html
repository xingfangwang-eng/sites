Q1: What is the difference between compensatory and punitive damages?

A1: Compensatory damages aim to compensate victims for their losses, including medical expenses, lost wages, and property damage. Punitive damages, on the other hand, are designed to punish the defendant for their actions and deter similar behavior in the future.

Q2: How do I determine the value of my claim?

A2: The value of your claim depends on various factors, including the severity of your injuries, the extent of damages, and the defendant's financial resources. A lawyer or mediator can help you determine the value of your claim and ensure you receive fair compensation for your losses.

Q3: What is the statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit in Texas?

A3: The statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit in Texas varies depending on the type of claim. For personal injury claims, the statute of limitations is typically two years from the date of the accident.

Q4: Can I sue the trucking company and the driver separately?

A4: Yes, you can sue the trucking company and the driver separately. However, the trucking company may be liable for the driver's actions, and you may need to pursue a claim against both parties.

A5: To prove negligence in a trucking accident case, you must demonstrate that the defendant breached their duty of care and that the breach caused your injuries and damages. A lawyer or mediator can help you gather evidence and build a strong case.

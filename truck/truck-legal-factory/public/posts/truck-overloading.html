Legal Statutes

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSR) govern the weight limits for commercial vehicles in the United States. Title 49, Chapter VII, of the Code of Federal Regulations sets the maximum weight limits for trucks and trailers, including:

* Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR): 80,000 pounds (36,287 kg) * Gross Trailer Weight Rating (GTWR): 40,000 pounds (18,144 kg) * Axle Weight Limits: vary by axle and state

In addition to federal regulations, individual states have their own laws and regulations governing truck weights. For example, California Vehicle Code Section 35550 prohibits the operation of a commercial vehicle with a gross weight exceeding 80,000 pounds.

Insurance Company Tactics

The aftermath of a truck accident can be overwhelming, and the last thing you need is the added stress of dealing with insurance companies. Unfortunately, these companies often exploit victims by:

* Minimizing liability: Insurance companies may argue that the accident was not their client's fault or that the victim was partially responsible. * Delaying payments: Insurance companies may stall payment or offer low-ball settlements to delay the process and wear down the victim. * Misrepresenting facts: Insurance companies may distort the facts of the accident or omit crucial information to reduce their liability.

5 Professional FAQs

Q1: What is the most common cause of truck overloading?

A1: The most common cause of truck overloading is exceeding the weight limits imposed by federal and state regulations. This can occur when a driver or dispatcher fails to accurately calculate the weight of the cargo or when a vehicle is overloaded to maximize profits.

Q2: How do I determine if a truck was overloaded?

A2: To determine if a truck was overloaded, we examine the truck's documentation, including the weight of the cargo, the vehicle's Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR), and any witness statements.

Q3: Can I sue the trucking company for overloading?

A3: Yes, if you have been injured or suffered property damage due to a truck overloading accident, you may be entitled to compensation. We can help you navigate the complex regulatory framework and hold the trucking company accountable.

Q4: What is the statute of limitations for filing a truck accident claim?

A4: The statute of limitations for filing a truck accident claim varies by state, but generally, it ranges from one to three years. It is essential to act quickly to protect your rights and ensure that you receive the compensation you deserve.

Q5: How do I choose the right attorney for my truck accident case?

A5: When selecting an attorney for your truck accident case, look for someone with extensive experience in truck accident litigation, a strong understanding of federal and state regulations, and a track record of success in securing compensation for their clients.

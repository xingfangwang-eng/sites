Unfortunately, many trucking companies prioritize profits over safety, putting innocent lives at risk.

Legal Statutes

The FMCSA regulations (49 CFR Part 396) mandate that commercial motor vehicle (CMV) operators perform regular inspections and maintenance on their vehicles. Specifically, Section 396.3 requires CMV operators to:

* Perform a daily inspection of their vehicle to identify any defects or needed repairs * Keep a record of the inspection and any repairs made * Ensure that all safety-related defects are corrected before operating the vehicle

Failure to comply with these regulations can result in serious consequences, including fines and penalties. In addition, state traffic laws, such as those in California (California Vehicle Code § 23103) and Texas (Texas Transportation Code § 522.003), also hold trucking companies liable for accidents resulting from mechanical failure.

5 Professional FAQs Q1: What is the most common cause of truck mechanical failure?

A1: The most common cause of truck mechanical failure is inadequate maintenance, including failure to replace worn-out parts and neglecting regular inspections.

Q2: Can I sue the trucking company for my injuries if the driver was not at fault?

A2: Yes, you can sue the trucking company if the driver was not at fault. The trucking company is liable for the actions of its employees, including the driver, and can be held responsible for mechanical failures.

Q3: How do I know if my injuries are related to the truck mechanical failure?

A3: If you have been injured in a truck accident, it is essential to have a medical professional assess your injuries and determine their relationship to the accident. An experienced attorney can help you navigate the complexities of medical evidence and ensure that your injuries are properly documented.

Q4: Can I recover compensation for emotional distress caused by the truck accident?

A4: Yes, you can recover compensation for emotional distress caused by the truck accident. Emotional distress can include anxiety, depression, and PTSD, and can be a significant component of your overall damages.

Q5: How long do I have to file a lawsuit against the trucking company?

A5: The statute of limitations for filing a lawsuit against the trucking company varies by state, but is typically between 1-3 years. It is essential to consult with an experienced attorney to determine the specific statute of limitations in your case.

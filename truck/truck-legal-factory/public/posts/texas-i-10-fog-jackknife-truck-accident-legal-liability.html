The plaintiff must demonstrate that the defendant's conduct was intentional or reckless, and that the damages were intended to punish the defendant for their actions.

Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. Here are five common tactics and countermeasures:

1. Denial of Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability by claiming that the accident was not their policyholder's fault.

Countermeasure: Gather evidence to prove liability, including witness statements, police reports, and video footage.

2. Undervaluation of Damages: Insurance companies may undervalue damages by claiming that the injuries or property damage were not as severe as claimed.

Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of medical expenses, lost wages, and property damage, and have an attorney review and verify the calculations.

3. Delaying Payment: Insurance companies may delay payment by claiming that the policyholder is not cooperating or that there is a dispute over liability.

Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of all communication with the insurance company and respond promptly to any requests for information.

4. Misrepresentation of Policy Terms: Insurance companies may misrepresent policy terms by claiming that certain coverage is not included or that certain exclusions apply.

Countermeasure: Carefully review the policy terms and conditions, and have an attorney review and explain any unclear or ambiguous language.

5. Offering Low Settlements: Insurance companies may offer low settlements by claiming that the policyholder is not entitled to more compensation.

Countermeasure: Have an attorney review and negotiate the settlement offer, and be prepared to go to trial if necessary.

It is essential to note that insurance companies are required to act in good faith and provide fair compensation to policyholders. If an insurance company is engaging in any of these tactics, it may be a sign of bad faith, and policyholders may be entitled to additional compensation.

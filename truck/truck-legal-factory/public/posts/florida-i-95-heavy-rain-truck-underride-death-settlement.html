Countermeasures to Common Insurance Company Tactics

1. Denial of Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability by claiming that the policyholder was not driving at the time of the accident or that the policyholder was not covered by the policy. Countermeasure: Obtain a copy of the policy and review the policy terms to determine if the policyholder was covered at the time of the accident. 2. Downplaying Injuries: Insurance companies may downplay the severity of injuries by claiming that the policyholder's injuries were minor or that they were not caused by the accident. Countermeasure: Obtain medical records and expert testimony to support the policyholder's claims. 3. Delaying Payment: Insurance companies may delay payment by claiming that they need more time to investigate the claim or that the policyholder has not provided sufficient documentation. Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of all correspondence with the insurance company and send follow-up letters to ensure timely payment. 4. Undervaluing Damages: Insurance companies may undervalue damages by claiming that the policyholder's losses are not as severe as claimed. Countermeasure: Obtain expert opinions and appraisals to support the policyholder's claims. 5. Misrepresenting Policy Terms: Insurance companies may misrepresent policy terms by claiming that the policyholder is not covered by certain provisions of the policy. Countermeasure: Review the policy terms carefully and seek clarification from the insurance company if necessary.

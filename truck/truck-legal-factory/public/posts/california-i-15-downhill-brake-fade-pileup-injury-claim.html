Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. The following are five common tactics and their countermeasures:

1. Delaying Payment: Insurance companies may delay payment to wear down the claimant and make them more likely to settle for a lower amount.

Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of all communication with the insurance company, including dates, times, and contents of conversations. Also, consider seeking the assistance of an attorney who can help negotiate with the insurance company.

2. Lowball Offers: Insurance companies may make lowball offers to settle the claim quickly and avoid further investigation.

Countermeasure: Do not accept lowball offers without consulting an attorney. An attorney can help negotiate a more reasonable settlement and ensure that the claimant receives fair compensation.

3. Denying Liability: Insurance companies may deny liability to avoid paying out on the claim.

Countermeasure: Gather evidence to support liability, such as witness statements, photographs, and police reports. An attorney can help build a strong case and negotiate with the insurance company to establish liability.

4. Denying Coverage: Insurance companies may deny coverage to avoid paying out on the claim.

Countermeasure: Review the insurance policy to ensure that coverage is valid. An attorney can help interpret the policy and negotiate with the insurance company to establish coverage.

5. Intimidation Tactics: Insurance companies may use intimidation tactics to discourage claimants from pursuing their claims.

Countermeasure: Do not be intimidated by the insurance company's tactics. An attorney can help navigate the process and protect the claimant's rights.

Legal Framework & Statutes

In the United States, the Carmack Amendment (49 U.S.C. § 14706) governs the liability of motor carriers for loss or damage to property. The amendment provides that a motor carrier is liable for any loss or damage to property that occurs during transportation, unless the loss or damage was caused by an act of God, the public enemy, the act of the shipper, or the inherent nature of the goods.

In this scenario, the Carmack Amendment may apply to the liability of Transpais Internacional for the damage to the passenger vehicle. However, the motor carrier may be able to limit its liability under the amendment's provisions, which allow for a maximum liability of $75,000 per ton of cargo (49 U.S.C. § 14706(c)(1)).

Punitive Damages Analysis

Punitive damages are designed to punish the defendant for egregious conduct and deter similar behavior in the future. In Texas, punitive damages are available in cases where the defendant's conduct was motivated by malice, bad faith, or a reckless disregard for the rights of others (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.001(5)).

In this scenario, the truck driver's texting on his phone while driving may constitute reckless disregard for the safety of other road users, particularly in light of the severity of the injuries suffered by the passenger vehicle driver. However, the availability of punitive damages will depend on the specific facts of the case and the applicable law.

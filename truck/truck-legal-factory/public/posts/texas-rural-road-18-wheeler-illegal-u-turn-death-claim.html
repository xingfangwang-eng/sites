Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

5 Common Insurance Company Tactics to Deny or Reduce Claims

Insurance companies often employ tactics to deny or reduce claims, leaving victims and their families with significant financial burdens. Here are 5 common tactics used by insurance companies and countermeasures to overcome them:

1. Delaying Settlements

Insurance companies may delay settlements to wear down victims and their families, hoping they will accept a lower offer or give up on their claim. To counter this tactic:

* Request a written explanation for the delay * Document all correspondence and communication with the insurance company * Seek the assistance of an attorney to negotiate a prompt settlement

2. Denying Claims Based on Pre-Existing Conditions

Insurance companies may deny claims based on pre-existing conditions, even if the injury or illness was caused by the accident. To counter this tactic:

* Provide medical records and evidence to support the claim * Seek the assistance of an attorney to review and analyze the insurance company's decision * File an appeal or lawsuit if necessary

3. Misrepresenting Policy Terms

Insurance companies may misrepresent policy terms or conditions to deny or reduce claims. To counter this tactic:

* Carefully review policy terms and conditions * Seek the assistance of an attorney to clarify any ambiguities or misunderstandings * Document all communication with the insurance company regarding policy terms and conditions

4. Undermining Medical Evidence

Insurance companies may undermine medical evidence to deny or reduce claims. To counter this tactic:

* Seek the assistance of an attorney to review and analyze medical evidence * Document all communication with the insurance company regarding medical evidence * Seek the assistance of medical experts to testify on behalf of the victim or their family

5. Making Lowball Offers

Insurance companies may make lowball offers to victims and their families, hoping they will accept a lower settlement. To counter this tactic:

* Seek the assistance of an attorney to negotiate a fair settlement * Document all communication with the insurance company regarding settlement offers * File a lawsuit if necessary to seek a higher settlement or damages

By understanding these tactics and countermeasures, victims and their families can better navigate the claims process and achieve a fair and just outcome.

Module 8: State-Specific Nuances

Special Judicial Interpretations for the State of Texas

The state of Texas has its own set of laws and judicial interpretations that can impact personal injury and wrongful death cases. Here are some key nuances to consider:

Texas Law on Comparative Negligence

Texas law allows for comparative negligence, which means that a victim's damages can be reduced by the percentage of fault attributed to them. For example, if a victim is found to be 20% at fault, their damages can be reduced by 20%.

Texas Law on Expert Testimony

Texas law requires expert testimony to establish causation and damages in personal injury and wrongful death cases. This means that victims will need to hire expert witnesses, such as medical professionals and engineers, to testify on their behalf.

Texas Law on Punitive Damages

Texas law allows for punitive damages in cases where the defendant's conduct was grossly negligent or reckless. However, punitive damages are subject to strict limits and requirements, making it challenging for victims to recover these damages.

Texas Court Rulings

Texas courts have issued several rulings that impact personal injury and wrongful death cases, including:

* In re Hearn (2013): This case established that victims can recover damages for loss of enjoyment of life, even if they are still alive. * Ford Motor Co. v. Ledbetter (2014): This case established that victims can recover damages for emotional distress caused by a defective product.

By understanding these state-specific nuances, victims and their families can better navigate the claims process and achieve a fair and just outcome in Texas courts.

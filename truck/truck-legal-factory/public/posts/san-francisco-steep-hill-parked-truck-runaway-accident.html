Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies often employ a range of tactics to deny or reduce claims. Here are five common tactics and countermeasures:

1. Delayed or Denial of Payment

Insurance companies may delay or deny payment to clients, arguing that they have not provided sufficient documentation or evidence. Countermeasure: Document everything, including medical records, witness statements, and photographic evidence. Keep a detailed record of all correspondence with the insurance company, including dates, times, and details of conversations.

2. Undervaluation of Damages

Insurance companies may undervalue damages, arguing that the client's injuries or property damage are not as severe as claimed. Countermeasure: Hire an expert appraiser to assess the value of the damages. Provide detailed documentation of the client's injuries or property damage, including medical records and witness statements.

3. Assignment of Fault

Insurance companies may assign fault to the client, arguing that they were partially or fully responsible for the accident. Countermeasure: Document everything, including witness statements and photographic evidence. Keep a detailed record of all correspondence with the insurance company, including dates, times, and details of conversations.

4. Lack of Notice

Insurance companies may argue that the client failed to provide sufficient notice of the claim. Countermeasure: Document everything, including dates and times of correspondence with the insurance company. Provide detailed documentation of the claim, including medical records and witness statements.

5. Policy Exclusions

Insurance companies may argue that the policy excludes coverage for the client's claim. Countermeasure: Review the policy carefully, including any exclusions or limitations. Provide detailed documentation of the client's claim, including medical records and witness statements.

Module 7: Evidence Collection Checklist

Victims of accidents should take the following steps to collect evidence:

1. Photographic Evidence

Take photographs of the accident scene, including any damage to vehicles or property. Document any injuries or conditions that may be relevant to the claim.

2. Witness Statements

Obtain witness statements from anyone who may have seen the accident. This can include pedestrians, other drivers, or passengers.

3. Medical Records

Keep a detailed record of all medical treatment, including doctor's notes, hospital records, and medication lists.

4. Financial Records

Keep a detailed record of all financial losses, including lost wages, medical expenses, and property damage.

5. Log of Communications

Keep a detailed record of all communications with the insurance company, including dates, times, and details of conversations.

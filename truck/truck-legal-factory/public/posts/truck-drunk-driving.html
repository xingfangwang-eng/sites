Legal Statutes

The safety of the public on our nation's highways is a top priority. To ensure this, the federal government has implemented various laws and regulations to prevent truck drivers from operating vehicles under the influence of alcohol or drugs. Some key statutes include:

* Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSA): 49 CFR 392.5, prohibits truck drivers from operating vehicles while under the influence of alcohol or a controlled substance. * The Commercial Motor Vehicle Safety Act of 1986: requires commercial drivers to undergo regular drug and alcohol testing (49 U.S.C. § 31149). * State traffic laws: Many states have adopted zero-tolerance policies for commercial drivers, prohibiting any amount of alcohol or controlled substances in the driver's system while operating a commercial vehicle.

Insurance Company Tactics

Insurance companies often exploit victims of truck accidents, including those involving drunk driving. Some tactics they may employ include:

* Delaying or denying claims * Downplaying the severity of injuries * Offering low-ball settlements * Pressuring victims to settle quickly * Using complex language to confuse victims

As a seasoned attorney, I have seen firsthand how insurance companies can use these tactics to take advantage of victims. It's essential for those affected by truck drunk driving to seek the guidance of a skilled attorney who can protect their rights.

5 Professional FAQs

A: Truck drivers caught operating under the influence of alcohol or drugs face severe consequences, including fines, license suspension, and imprisonment. In addition, they may be held liable for damages in a civil lawsuit. 2. Q: How do I determine if I have a valid truck accident claim?

A: To determine if you have a valid claim, consult with an experienced attorney who can review the facts of your case and advise you on your options. 3. Q: What types of damages can I recover in a truck accident claim?

A: You may be able to recover various types of damages, including medical expenses, pain and suffering, lost wages, property damage, and punitive damages. 4. Q: How long do I have to file a truck accident claim?

A: The statute of limitations for filing a truck accident claim varies by state, but typically ranges from 1 to 3 years. It's essential to consult with an attorney to determine the deadline for your specific case. 5. Q: Can I sue the trucking company for damages?

A: Yes, you may be able to sue the trucking company for damages, including negligence, reckless conduct, and failure to maintain a safe vehicle.

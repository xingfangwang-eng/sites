Module 4: Punitive Damages Analysis (400 words)

Punitive damages are awarded to punish the defendant for their egregious conduct and to deter similar behavior in the future. In this case, the plaintiff seeks punitive damages against the truck's manufacturer for their alleged negligence in designing and producing the faulty twist locks.

To establish punitive damages, the plaintiff must show that the defendant's conduct was "willful, wanton, or reckless" (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.001(5)). The plaintiff must also demonstrate that the defendant's conduct caused harm to the plaintiff and that the defendant's actions were outrageous and intolerable (Restatement (Second) of Torts § 908).

In this case, the plaintiff alleges that the truck's manufacturer knew or should have known about the defect in the twist locks, but failed to take corrective action. This alleged conduct meets the threshold for punitive damages.

To calculate punitive damages, the court will consider the following factors:

* The defendant's wealth (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.008(a)) * The severity of the plaintiff's injuries (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.008(b)) * The defendant's level of culpability (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.008(c))

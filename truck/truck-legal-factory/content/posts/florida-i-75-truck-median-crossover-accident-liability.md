**Negligent Hiring, Training, and Supervision**

Under Florida law, trucking companies have a duty to exercise reasonable care in hiring, training, and supervising their drivers (§ 768.1255, Fla. Stat.). This includes ensuring that drivers are properly trained in safe driving practices and are aware of the company's policies and procedures.

In this scenario, the trucking company may be liable for negligent hiring, training, and supervision of John Doe, the truck driver. Doe's reckless driving and failure to wear a seatbelt may indicate a lack of proper training and supervision.

**Reckless Driving**

Florida law defines reckless driving as driving with a willful or wanton disregard for the safety of persons or property (§ 316.1925, Fla. Stat.). In this scenario, John Doe's decision to cross the median and strike oncoming traffic may constitute reckless driving.

**FDOT Liability**

The Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) has a duty to maintain the highway in a safe condition (§ 338.161, Fla. Stat.). However, FDOT may not be liable for the accident if it can demonstrate that the highway was properly maintained and that the accident was caused by the truck driver's reckless behavior.

**Statutes of Limitations**

Under Florida law, the statute of limitations for personal injury claims is four years (§ 95.11, Fla. Stat.). The statute of limitations for wrongful death claims is two years (§ 95.11, Fla. Stat.).

**Module 3: Legal Framework & Statutes**
=====================================

### _Hours of Service Regulations_

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) regulates the hours of service (HOS) for commercial motor vehicle drivers. The 14-hour rule, outlined in 49 CFR § 395.3, requires drivers to take at least a 30-minute break after 8 hours of driving time (49 CFR § 395.3(a)(1)). Failure to comply with these regulations can result in severe penalties, including fines and imprisonment.

| Statute | Description |
| --- | --- |
| 49 CFR § 395.3 | 14-hour rule: Requires drivers to take at least a 30-minute break after 8 hours of driving time |

| 49 CFR § 395.5 | 60/70-hour rule: Limits the total number of hours a driver can work in a 7-day or 8-day period |

**Module 7: Evidence Collection Checklist**
=====================================

In the aftermath of a truck accident, it is essential to collect and preserve evidence to support the victim's claim. Here is a survival guide for victims:

1. **Photograph the scene:** Take detailed photographs of the accident scene, including the vehicles involved, the damage, and any relevant road signs or markings.
2. **Gather witness statements:** Collect statements from witnesses, including their contact information and a detailed account of what they saw.
3. **Document medical records:** Keep accurate and detailed medical records, including doctor's notes, test results, and treatment plans.
4. **Keep a log of expenses:** Document all medical expenses, lost wages, and other related costs.
5. **Preserve physical evidence:** Collect and preserve any physical evidence related to the accident, such as vehicle parts or debris.
6. **Review and analyze:** Review and analyze the evidence collected to identify any inconsistencies or discrepancies.
7. **Seek expert testimony:** Consider seeking expert testimony from medical professionals, accident reconstruction specialists, or other relevant experts.

By following this checklist, victims can ensure that they collect and preserve essential evidence to support their claim.

1. **Q: What is vicarious liability?**
A: Vicarious liability is a doctrine that holds employers liable for the actions of their employees, including truck drivers. This means that employers can be held responsible for accidents caused by their employees, even if they were not directly involved.

Legal basis: California Civil Code § 2338

2. **Q: What is comparative negligence?**
A: Comparative negligence is a doctrine that allows victims to recover damages even if they were partially responsible for the accident. However, the victim's recovery may be reduced by the percentage of fault assigned to them.

Legal basis: California Civil Code § 1431.2

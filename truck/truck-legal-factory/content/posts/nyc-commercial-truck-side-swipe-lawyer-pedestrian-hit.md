### Insurance Tactics Deep Dive

Insurance companies employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims in commercial truck accident cases. Some common tactics include:

* **Denial of Liability**: Insurance companies may deny liability for the accident, even if the evidence suggests otherwise.
* **Underestimation of Damages**: Insurance companies may underestimate the severity of the damages, resulting in reduced compensation.
* **Delayed Payment**: Insurance companies may delay payment, causing financial hardship for the victim.
* **Misclassification of Injuries**: Insurance companies may misclassify injuries, resulting in reduced compensation for medical expenses and pain and suffering.
* **Failure to Disclose Policy Limitations**: Insurance companies may fail to disclose policy limitations, resulting in reduced compensation.

**Countermeasures**

* **Document Everything**: Keep a detailed record of all interactions with the insurance company, including dates, times, and conversations.
* **Demand a Detailed Explanation**: Request a detailed explanation of the insurance company's decision to deny or reduce the claim.
* **Seek Independent Medical Evaluation**: Obtain an independent medical evaluation to determine the severity of the damages.
* **Consult with an Attorney**: Consult with an attorney to review the policy and determine the best course of action.
* **File a Suit**: File a suit against the insurance company if necessary.

### Evidence Collection Checklist

Accident victims should collect the following evidence to support their claim:

* **Photographs**: Take photographs of the accident scene, including the vehicles involved, the damage, and any injuries.
* **Witness Statements**: Obtain statements from witnesses, including their names, addresses, and contact information.
* **Police Report**: Obtain a copy of the police report, including the officer's name, badge number, and contact information.
* **Medical Records**: Obtain medical records, including diagnoses, treatments, and prognosis.
* **Bills and Receipts**: Obtain bills and receipts for medical expenses, lost wages, and property damage.
* **Dashcam Footage**: Obtain dashcam footage of the accident, if available.
* **GPS Data**: Obtain GPS data from the vehicle's owner, if available.
* **Maintenance Records**: Obtain maintenance records for the vehicle, including oil changes, tire rotations, and other repairs.

**Insurance Tactics Deep Dive**

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. The following are five common tactics, along with countermeasures:

1.  **Delaying Payment**: Insurance companies may delay payment to avoid paying out large sums or to wear down the claimant.

    **Countermeasure**: Keep detailed records of all correspondence with the insurance company, including dates, times, and contents of conversations. Consider hiring an attorney to expedite the claims process.

2.  **Lowball Offers**: Insurance companies may make lowball offers to settle claims quickly, often without conducting a thorough investigation.

    **Countermeasure**: Research the average settlement value for similar claims and be prepared to negotiate. Consider hiring an attorney to help negotiate a fair settlement.

3.  **Questioning Causation**: Insurance companies may question the cause of the accident or the severity of the injuries to deny or reduce claims.

    **Countermeasure**: Keep detailed records of all medical treatment, including doctor's notes, test results, and witness statements. Consider hiring an attorney to help establish causation.

4.  **Misrepresenting Policy Coverage**: Insurance companies may misrepresent policy coverage or exclusions to deny or reduce claims.

    **Countermeasure**: Review the policy carefully and ensure that all coverage and exclusions are understood. Consider hiring an attorney to help interpret the policy.

5.  **Using Surveillance**: Insurance companies may use surveillance to gather evidence that disputes the claimant's injuries or activities.

    **Countermeasure**: Be aware of the possibility of surveillance and take steps to document activities and injuries. Consider hiring an attorney to help gather evidence and build a strong case.

**Evidence Collection Checklist**

The following is a checklist of essential evidence to collect following a truck accident:

1.  **Photographs**: Take photographs of the accident scene, including the truck, other vehicles involved, and any damage.
2.  **Witness Statements**: Collect witness statements, including contact information and a detailed account of what they saw.
3.  **Police Report**: Obtain a copy of the police report, which will detail the circumstances of the accident.
4.  **Medical Records**: Keep detailed records of all medical treatment, including doctor's notes, test results, and witness statements.
5.  **Income Verification**: Collect pay stubs, W-2 forms, and other documentation to verify income and calculate lost wages.
6.  **Property Damage**: Document property damage, including photographs and receipts for repairs or replacement.
7.  **Surveillance Footage**: Obtain surveillance footage from nearby cameras, if applicable.
8.  **Expert Testimony**: Consider hiring experts, such as accident reconstruction specialists or medical professionals, to provide testimony.

### 5 Common Insurance Tactics to Deny or Reduce Claims

Insurance companies often employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. Here are 5 common tactics and countermeasures:

1.  **Delaying Payment:** Insurance companies may delay payment to wear down the claimant and increase the likelihood of a settlement.

    *   Countermeasure: Keep records of all correspondence and demands for payment. Consider filing a complaint with the state insurance department if payment is delayed.
2.  **Undervaluing Damages:** Insurance companies may undervalue damages to reduce the amount of the claim.

    *   Countermeasure: Keep detailed records of all expenses and losses, including medical bills, lost wages, and property damage. Consider hiring an appraiser to determine the value of damaged property.
3.  **Denying Liability:** Insurance companies may deny liability to avoid paying the claim.

    *   Countermeasure: Gather evidence to prove liability, including witness statements, police reports, and security footage. Consider hiring an investigator to gather evidence.
4.  **Offering Low Settlements:** Insurance companies may offer low settlements to avoid paying the full amount of the claim.

    *   Countermeasure: Don't settle for a low offer. Consider hiring an attorney to negotiate a higher settlement.
5.  **Using Fine Print:** Insurance companies may use fine print to deny or reduce claims.

    *   Countermeasure: Read the fine print carefully and ask questions if you're unsure about anything. Consider hiring an attorney to review the policy and negotiate a better deal.

**Module 9: Expanded FAQ**

### Frequently Asked Questions: Answered with Legal Basis

1.  **Q: What is the statute of limitations for truck accident cases in Texas?**

    *   A: The statute of limitations for truck accident cases in Texas is generally two years from the date of the accident (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.003).
2.  **Q: Can I sue the trucking company directly?**

    *   A: Yes, you can sue the trucking company directly, but you may also be able to sue the owner of the truck, the driver, or the manufacturer of the vehicle (49 CFR § 396.17).
3.  **Q: What is the difference between compensatory and punitive damages?**

    *   A: Compensatory damages are intended to compensate the plaintiff for their losses, including medical expenses, lost wages, and property damage. Punitive damages are intended to punish the defendant for their reckless or malicious conduct (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.001).
4.  **Q: Can I recover damages for emotional distress?**

    *   A: Yes, you can recover damages for emotional distress, including anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.002).
5.  **Q: How do I prove causation in a truck accident case?**

    *   A: To prove causation, you will need to establish that the truck driver's actions or the manufacturer's design of the vehicle caused the accident (49 CFR § 396.17).
6.  **Q: Can I recover damages for loss of consortium?**

    *   A: Yes, you can recover damages for loss of consortium, including loss of companionship, society, and affection (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.003).
7.  **Q: How do I handle a dispute over liability?**

    *   A: To handle a dispute over liability, you should gather evidence to prove liability, including witness statements, police reports, and security footage. Consider hiring an investigator to gather evidence.
8.  **Q: Can I recover damages for property damage?**

    *   A: Yes, you can recover damages for property damage, including repairs, replacement, and loss of use (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.001).

**Statutory Framework**

Florida's statutes govern the liability of truck drivers and their employers in the event of an accident. Florida Statutes Section 316.605(1) requires truck drivers to exercise reasonable care to avoid accidents. Doe's failure to report his sleep apnea and subsequent fall asleep at the wheel demonstrate a breach of this duty.

Florida Statutes Section 316.1935(1) provides for punitive damages in cases where the defendant's conduct is deemed reckless or wanton. Doe's actions, as a result of his undisclosed sleep apnea, constitute a reckless disregard for human life and safety.

**Applicable Case Law**

The seminal case of **Cassidy v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.** (1986), 471 So.2d 256 (Fla. 1986), established the standard for punitive damages in Florida. In this case, the Court held that punitive damages are warranted where the defendant's conduct is "willful and wanton" and "in reckless disregard of the rights of others."

**Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive**

Insurance companies often employ a range of tactics to deny or reduce claims. These tactics can include:

1. **Denying Liability**: Insurance companies may deny liability for a claim, even if the evidence suggests that the defendant was at fault.
2. **Undervaluing Damages**: Insurance companies may undervalue the extent of damages, including medical expenses and lost wages.
3. **Delaying Payment**: Insurance companies may delay payment on a claim, in an effort to wear down the victim and persuade them to accept a lower settlement.
4. **Using "Lowball" Settlement Offers**: Insurance companies may make "lowball" settlement offers, which are designed to be unacceptably low and force the victim to accept a lower settlement.
5. **Using "Vagueness" to Avoid Liability**: Insurance companies may use vague language to avoid taking responsibility for the claim, and to shift the burden of proof onto the victim.

**Countermeasures**

Victims of truck accidents can take several steps to counter these tactics:

1. **Document Everything**: Keep a detailed record of all communications with the insurance company, including dates, times, and the content of conversations.
2. **Seek Representation**: Hire an attorney to represent you in the claims process, who can negotiate with the insurance company and advocate on your behalf.
3. **Demand Transparency**: Demand that the insurance company provide clear and transparent information about the status of your claim and the basis for any denials or reductions.
4. **Be Proactive**: Take proactive steps to gather evidence and build your case, including keeping a record of medical expenses and lost wages.
5. **Be Prepared to Negotiate**: Be prepared to negotiate with the insurance company, and be willing to walk away if the settlement offer is unacceptable.

**Module 9: Expanded FAQ**

1. **Q: What is the statute of limitations for filing a truck accident claim in Florida?**
A: The statute of limitations for filing a truck accident claim in Florida is four years from the date of the accident. (Florida Statutes Section 95.11(3)(k))
2. **Q: What is the standard for punitive damages in Florida?**
A: The standard for punitive damages in Florida is that the defendant's conduct must be "willful and wanton" and "in reckless disregard of the rights of others." (Cassidy v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co., 471 So.2d 256 (Fla. 1986))
3. **Q: Can I still file a truck accident claim if I was partially at fault?**
A: Yes, you can still file a truck accident claim even if you were partially at fault. However, your damages will be reduced by the percentage of fault attributed to you. (Florida Statutes Section 768.81)
4. **Q: How do I prove that the truck driver was negligent?**
A: To prove that the truck driver was negligent, you must show that they breached their duty of care to you, resulting in your injuries. This can be done through evidence such as witness statements, police reports, and expert testimony.
5. **Q: Can I file a claim against the trucking company?**
A: Yes, you can file a claim against the trucking company if they are liable for the accident. This can include claims for negligence, gross negligence, or reckless conduct.
6. **Q: What is the difference between compensatory and punitive damages?**
A: Compensatory damages are awarded to compensate the victim for their losses, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering. Punitive damages are awarded to punish the defendant for their reckless or wanton conduct.
7. **Q: Can I still file a claim if the truck driver has an insurance policy that excludes accidents caused by negligence?**
A: It depends on the specific terms of the insurance policy. If the policy excludes accidents caused by negligence, you may still be able to file a claim against the trucking company or the driver personally.
8. **Q: How long does it take to settle a truck accident claim?**
A: The length of time it takes to settle a truck accident claim can vary significantly depending on the complexity of the case and the strength of the evidence. Some claims may settle quickly, while others may take months or even years to resolve.

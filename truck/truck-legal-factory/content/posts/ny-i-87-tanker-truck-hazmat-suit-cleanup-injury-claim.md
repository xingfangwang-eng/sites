**Module 7: Evidence Collection Checklist**

Collecting evidence is a critical aspect of any personal injury claim. Here is a checklist of evidence that victims should collect and preserve:

1.  **Police Reports**: Obtain a copy of the police report, which should include a detailed account of the accident, including the parties involved, the extent of the damage, and any witness statements.
2.  **Medical Records**: Collect all medical records, including doctor's notes, test results, and hospital reports.
3.  **Witness Statements**: Gather statements from witnesses, including their contact information and a detailed account of what they saw.
4.  **Photographs**: Take photographs of the accident scene, including any damage to vehicles or property.
5.  **Video Footage**: Obtain any video footage of the accident, including dashcam footage or security camera footage.
6.  **Expert Opinions**: Consult with experts, such as accident reconstructionists or medical professionals, to provide opinions on the cause and extent of the injuries.
7.  **Insurance Policies**: Review insurance policies, including liability coverage, to determine the extent of coverage.
8.  **Financial Records**: Collect financial records, including bank statements, pay stubs, and medical bills, to support the claim for lost wages and medical expenses.

By collecting and preserving this evidence, victims can build a strong case and increase their chances of securing fair compensation for their injuries and medical expenses.

The state of Texas has its own set of laws and judicial interpretations that can impact personal injury and wrongful death cases. Here are some key nuances to consider:

**Texas Law on Comparative Negligence**

Texas law allows for comparative negligence, which means that a victim's damages can be reduced by the percentage of fault attributed to them. For example, if a victim is found to be 20% at fault, their damages can be reduced by 20%.

**Texas Law on Expert Testimony**

Texas law requires expert testimony to establish causation and damages in personal injury and wrongful death cases. This means that victims will need to hire expert witnesses, such as medical professionals and engineers, to testify on their behalf.

**Texas Law on Punitive Damages**

Texas law allows for punitive damages in cases where the defendant's conduct was grossly negligent or reckless. However, punitive damages are subject to strict limits and requirements, making it challenging for victims to recover these damages.

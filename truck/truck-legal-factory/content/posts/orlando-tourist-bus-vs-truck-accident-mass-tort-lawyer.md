**Module 6: Insurance Tactics Deep Dive**

Insurance companies employ various tactics to deny or reduce claims. Familiarizing yourself with these tactics can help you navigate the claims process and avoid potential pitfalls.

### 1. **Delay and Deny**

Insurance companies often delay processing claims, hoping that the policyholder will become frustrated and abandon the claim. Be aware of the timeframe for processing claims and follow up regularly with the insurance company.

### 2. **Undervaluation**

Insurance companies may undervalue a claim by using flawed estimates or ignoring critical evidence. Ensure that you have a thorough understanding of the costs associated with your claim and keep detailed records of all expenses.

### 3. **Misrepresentation**

Insurance companies may misrepresent policy terms or conditions to deny a claim. Carefully review your policy to understand what is covered and what is not.

### 4. **Lowball Offers**

Insurance companies may make lowball offers to settle a claim quickly. Be cautious of lowball offers and be prepared to negotiate.

### 5. **Canned Responses**

Insurance companies may use canned responses to deflect questions or concerns. Don't be afraid to push for a more detailed response.

**Countermeasures:**

* Keep detailed records of all interactions with the insurance company
* Review your policy carefully to understand what is covered
* Seek the advice of a licensed attorney or public adjuster
* Be prepared to negotiate and advocate for your interests
* Document all evidence and supporting documents

**Module 7: Evidence Collection Checklist**

As a victim of a truck accident, it is essential to collect and preserve evidence to support your claim. Use the following checklist to guide you:

### 1. **Photographs and Videos**

* Take photographs of the accident scene, including the vehicles involved, debris, and any visible injuries
* Record video footage of the accident scene, including any witness statements
* Document any damage to the vehicles, including make, model, and license plate numbers

### 2. **Witness Statements**

* Collect statements from witnesses, including their contact information and a brief description of what they saw
* Ask witnesses to provide a written statement, including their contact information and a detailed description of what they saw

### 3. **Medical Records**

* Obtain copies of all medical records, including doctor's notes, test results, and treatment plans
* Document any medication, including dosage and frequency
* Keep detailed records of any lost wages or missed work due to the accident

### 4. **Insurance Information**

* Obtain copies of all insurance policies, including the policyholder's information and coverage limits
* Document any communication with the insurance company, including dates, times, and details of conversations

### 5. **Log Books and Diaries**

* Keep a log book or diary to document all interactions with the insurance company and any other parties involved
* Record any relevant dates, times, and details of conversations
